Load a TuneBook

Book > Import TuneBook
Import an Abc Tunebook from your local disc or from your Google Drive.

Book > {{exampleFileNameWithoutAbc}}
Load an example Tunebook from the server. Martin's Tunebook contains Irish Tunes with Fingering for Piano Accordion. For more information please visit Martin's BoxPlayer Talks and Martin's Irish Traditional Music YouTube Channel


Add Abc tunes

Copy an Abc-tune to the clipboard , e.g. from session.org. Don't copy the first line which starts with 'X:'. eTuneBook generates the X-line automatically.

Book > New
Generate a new Tunebook with one new tune.

Tunes > New
Add a new tune.

In both cases the Abc-Editor for the new Tune opens. Paste the Abc-tune from the clipboard to the Abc-Editor. The dots are shown simultanously on the left side.

Your edits are saved as soon as you leave the Abc-Editor (by clicking somewhere else).


Export Abc tunes

Book > Export TuneBook
Displays the TuneBook in Abc-format.

The system automatically writes the TuneBook to a file on your device. The TuneBook is named 'MyTuneBook.txt' and gets placed in the Download Folder of your Browser. Rename the TuneBook and give it the file-extension .abc (otherwise you won't be able to re-import it to eTuneBook).

By clicking Export to local Drive eTuneBook writes your TuneBook to a file on your device. The TuneBook is named 'MyTuneBook.txt' and gets placed in the Download Folder of your Browser. Rename the TuneBook and give it the file-extension .abc (otherwise you won't be able to re-import it to eTuneBook).

By clicking Export to Google Drive you can upload your TuneBook to your Google Drive.

Choose which of the eTuneBook-specific fields should be exported by toggling the corresponding export selectors.

Attention: If you choose to export only standard Abc eTuneBook-specific fields will be lost after Re-Import.


Edit or delete Abc tunes

Choose a tune and then click Abc. Edit the tune whithin the Abc-Editor (Tune-Editor).

Your edits are saved as soon as you leave the Abc-Editor (by clicking somewhere else).

In case you want to get rid of the tune, just delete the whole Abc-Code of the tune.

In case you want to sort your tunes by modification date, you might consider to include a line starting with 'H:' and also contains 'Modified' followed by the modification date in form of 'yyyy-mm-dd'. Check Martin's Tunebook for examples.

Choose Note Editor in order to change single notes by choosing form a a given selection of values. A click on a note will load the current values of that note into the Dropdown lists.


Build Tune Sets

In eTuneBook every tune belongs to one or more set. If you load an Abc-TuneBook that was generated by another program or by hand, eTuneBook puts every tune in a separate set. Also new tunes are first put into a new set.

In order to move or copy a tune from one set to another, choose a set and then choose Move from the Menu on the right side of the Tune title.

On the Move-Panel choose where to move: before or after another tune. Choose whether the Tune should be moved or copied.

After pressing 'Send' on the Move-Panel eTuneBook puts the tune at the chosen position in the target set and closes the Move-Panel.

In case your choice was 'move', eTuneBook removes the tune from the original set.

In case your choice was 'copy', eTuneBook does not remove the tune from the original set.

In order to delete a tune from a set, choose a set and then choose Delete from the Menu on the right side of the Tune title.

If after Deleting from the set the tune gets set-less (no other sets), the system automatically generates a new set around the tune.

In case the original set gets empty after moving or deleting a tune, the empty set will be automatically removed.

In case a tune exists in more than one set, choosing sets in the tune view shows a list of all sets containing the tune, staying in the tune view. In order to build a new set click on New.

In case a tune exists in only one set, choosing sets in the tune view changes to the set view. In order to build a new set here, move the tune (using the Move Panel as described above) to an undefined (empty) destination set.


Edit TuneSet-Position Info

Choose a set and then choose Edit from the Menu on the right side of the Tune title.

You might add the following informations to the chosen TuneSet-Position:

  • Repeat: How many times the tune should be played (default is 3x).
  • Comment: Additional informations about how to play the tune.

Build Playlists

Click on the button Playlists

Choose New above the list of all playlists.

Add a new playlist:

  • Name (e.g. venue)
  • Event (e.g. concert or ceili or session)
  • Band

Choose a playlist and click New above the playlist positions.

Add a new set:

  • Name: Sets carry by default the name of the first tune. You might give another name here (e.g. a Set-Dance-Set name).
  • Set: Choose the set here
  • Comment: Add a comment specific for the new playlist position

Move a Set up/down in the playlist.


Mark a Tune as 'Just Played'

Choose a tune and click Played in the Tune View. This adds the current date and time to the tune.

Alternatively you might choose a set and click Played on a TuneSet-Position in the Set View. This adds the current date and time to the tune.

In case you just played the whole set, click Just Played in the Set View or the Playlist View. This adds the current date and time to all of the tunes in the set.

eTuneBook calculates a playing frequency from all playing dates: Each play date gets points. For example: 1000 days ago: 0 Points, 999 days ago: 1 Point, 100 days ago: 900 points, 1 day ago: 999 Points. The playing frequency of the tune is the sum of the frequency scores of all playing dates.


Colorize a Tune

Choose a tune and click on the square on the right-side of color in the Tune Editor.

Once chosen a color it gets added to the tune.


Transpose a Tune

Choose a tune and click on the arrows on the right-side of transpose in the Tune Editor.

Transposition changes the Abc-Code of the tune. Changes are effective in the dots, and in case the Abc-Editor is open, in the Abc-Code.


Edit Tune Info

Choose Info in the Tune View.

You might add the following informations to the chosen Tune:

  • Tune Sites: External web sites holding informations about the tune.
  • Comment: Your own comments about the tune.

Managing Tune Sites:

  • Click New to add a new Tune Site.
  • Click into the URL field in order to Edit the Tune Site.
  • Click on Delete in order to delete the Tune Site.
  • Click on Play in order to open the Tune Site in another window.

Edit Tune Videos

Choose a tune and click Videos in the Tune View.

  • Click New to add a new Tune Video.
  • Click into Code or Description in order to Edit the Tune Video.
  • Click on Delete in order to delete the Tune Video.
  • Clicking on a Tune Video Record opens the video below the video list.

eTuneBook currently supports YouTube Videos. The Code field holds only the video-specific part of the video-url, not the whole adress. Check Martin's Tunebook for examples.


Printing

Use the your Browser's printing functionality (ctrl p) in order to print the page your viewing.


Searching Sets and Tunes

Tunes and Sets can be filtered by:

  • Tune Title

  • Tune Playing Range

  • Tune Modification Range

  • Tune Frequency Range

  • Tune Type

  • Tune Key

  • Tune Color

  • Tune Skill

  • Playlist Event

  • Playlist Band

Tunes can be sorted by:

  • Title

  • Type

  • Key

  • Last Play Date

  • Playing Frequency

  • Skill

  • Last Modification Date

Click on the column header in order to sort the column.
